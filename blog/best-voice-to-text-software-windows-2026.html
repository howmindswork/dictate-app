Privacy: The Factor Most Tools Hide

Otter.ai stores your audio on their servers. Wispr Flow routes voice through the cloud. Even Windows built-in dictation sends data to Microsoft.

dictate.app processes everything locally. Your audio never leaves your machine. No logs. No uploads. No risk.

For lawyers, doctors, therapists, and anyone handling sensitive information, this is not optional.

Speed: 150 WPM vs Your Typing Speed

Average typing speed is 60 words per minute. Fast typists hit 90. Voice dictation at 150 WPM is not marketing - it is physics. You speak faster than you type. Period.

dictate.app transcribes in under a second after you stop speaking. Powered by Groq Whisper, one of the fastest inference engines available in 2026.

Dragon Dictate: Why It is Still Around

Dragon Professional has a legacy. Twenty years of training data. A loyal user base. But it costs $150+ upfront, the UI is dated, and cloud features require subscriptions on top.

dictate.app delivers comparable accuracy at a fraction of the cost. $8.99/month vs $150+. No contest for new users.

Windows Built-in Dictation: Good Enough?

Windows 11 has a passable built-in dictation tool. It requires internet. It works in some apps and breaks in others. You cannot change the hotkey. And Microsoft receives your audio.

For occasional use, it is fine. For anyone dictating more than 10 minutes a day, it falls short.
