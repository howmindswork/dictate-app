BYOK Voice Dictation for Windows

What it is, why it matters, and how to get true privacy in dictation

What is BYOK?

BYOK = Bring Your Own Key. Instead of a dictation app controlling access to the transcription engine, you provide your own API key.

With dictate.app's BYOK model, you bring a Groq API key. Your audio goes straight from your PC to Groq's servers. The dictate.app desktop client is just a keyboard shortcut manager—it records audio, sends it directly to Groq, and types the result at your cursor.

BYOK Flow (dictate.app):
Your PC → [Record WAV] → Groq API (your key) → Transcription → Your cursor

Non-BYOK Flow (typical):
Your PC → [Record audio] → DictaApp servers → Groq/OpenAI → DictaApp → Your cursor

Why BYOK Matters for Privacy

In traditional dictation apps, your audio bounces through a middle server. That middle server is:

  • A data collection point. Servers log audio, metadata, timestamps, IP addresses. Even if encrypted in transit, it sits in someone's data center.
  • A compliance liability. Healthcare professionals, lawyers, and government workers face HIPAA, attorney-client privilege, and classified information rules. Server routing adds complexity.
  • A single point of failure. If the dictation company gets hacked, your audio is exposed. If they go out of business, your data handling is uncertain.

With BYOK, you eliminate the middle server. Your audio goes directly to the transcription provider (Groq, in dictate.app's case). You control the API key. You own the relationship with Groq.

dictate.app never touches your audio. We don't see it, store it, or have access to it. We see only metadata: that transcription happened, how many tokens were used (for your Groq billing), and nothing else.

Groq Whisper API: The Engine

dictate.app uses Groq Whisper as its transcription engine. Groq is a specialized AI inference company—their hardware is optimized for speed. Whisper is OpenAI's speech-to-text model running on Groq's infrastructure.

Result: ~200ms latency from audio end to transcript.

Compare that to OpenAI Whisper API directly (~15–30 seconds) or Google Speech-to-Text (~5–10 seconds). Groq's speed comes from hardware optimization, not software magic.

How to Set Up BYOK Dictation on Windows

  1. Get a Groq API key. Visit groq.com, sign up, grab your free API key (or use a paid tier for production).
  2. Install dictate.app. Download from dictate.app, install, launch.
  3. Paste your Groq key. dictate.app prompts for your API key on first launch.
  4. Press Ctrl+Shift+Space. Hold. Speak. Release. Text appears at your cursor.

Common Questions

Q: Does Groq sell my audio data? No. Groq's terms don't allow them to retain or train on your transcriptions. Your audio is processed and discarded.
