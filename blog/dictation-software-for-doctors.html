The Dragon Medical Problem

Dragon Medical Professional is the gold standard for medical dictation. It handles clinical terminology well. It has been around for decades. And it costs $2,000 to $3,000 per year per physician.

For a solo practitioner or small practice, that is a serious line item. For residents and early-career physicians, it is often simply not affordable.

Dragon also requires dedicated training time, a local installation that can conflict with hospital IT policies, and periodic retraining to maintain accuracy.

How dictate.app Works for Medical Documentation

dictate.app is a Windows app that transcribes speech using Groq's Whisper API. You hold a hotkey, speak, release. Your words appear wherever your cursor is.

That means it works in:

  • Epic (browser or desktop client)
  • Cerner (any text field)
  • Athenahealth
  • Any browser-based EHR
  • Microsoft Word (for offline documentation)
  • Outlook (for patient communications and referral letters)

If the EHR has a text field, dictate.app can put text in it. That covers every EHR system on the market.

Privacy and Audio Handling

dictate.app sends audio to Groq's API for transcription. The audio is discarded after processing. dictate.app does not store, log, or retain audio recordings.

For clinical environments with specific compliance requirements, consult your institution's compliance officer before adopting any voice transcription tool. This applies to Dragon Medical and every other cloud-assisted dictation product as well.

The push-to-talk model also helps: only what you actively dictate gets sent. You are not broadcasting a room conversation. You hold the key, speak the documentation, release. Only that audio is processed.

Clinical Use Cases

Progress Notes

The highest-volume documentation task. Dictate the SOAP note structure, history, assessment, and plan. At 150 WPM speaking vs 50 WPM typing, a 300-word note takes 2 minutes instead of 6.

Referral Letters

Specialist referral letters are time-consuming to type. Dictate the clinical summary, relevant history, and specific questions for the consultant. What takes 20 minutes of typing takes 7 minutes of speaking.

Patient Instructions

After-visit summaries, medication instructions, follow-up plans. Dictate directly into the patient instruction field in the EHR. Done before the patient leaves the exam room.

Discharge Summaries

Hospital discharge documentation is notoriously time-consuming. Dictating the summary while reviewing the chart is faster than alternating between reading and typing.
