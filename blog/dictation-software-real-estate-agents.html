Best Dictation Software for Real Estate Agents

May 2026  ·  7 min read

Real estate agents write more than almost anyone in their workday. Property descriptions. Client emails. Offer letters. CRM notes from showings. MLS listing copy. Follow-up messages after open houses.

A busy agent easily writes 2,000 words a day. At 45 words per minute, that is 44 minutes of typing. Speaking at 140 words per minute, it is 14 minutes. The 30-minute difference compounds across a year into roughly 130 hours.

Voice dictation is the obvious fix. Here is what to use.

The Writing Load on Real Estate Agents

Let us be specific about where the words go:

  • Property descriptions: 150-400 words per listing. A high-volume agent lists 3-5 properties per month.
  • Client emails: 20-40 per day across buyers, sellers, lenders, attorneys, and other agents.
  • CRM notes: Notes from every showing, every call, every offer conversation.
  • Offer letters and disclosures: Long documents with significant repetition across transactions.
  • Social media: Listing announcements, market updates, open house invites.

None of this requires creative typing skill. It requires getting information from your brain onto a screen as fast as possible. Voice dictation is purpose-built for that.

What dictate.app Does

dictate.app is a push-to-talk voice dictation app for Windows 10 and 11. You hold a hotkey, speak, and release. In about 200 milliseconds, transcribed text appears at your cursor.

It works in every Windows application that accepts a paste command. MLS portals, CRM systems, Gmail, Outlook, Word, your brokerage's internal tools. Anywhere you can click and type, you can click and speak instead.

Push-to-talk is the right design for real estate. You are not dictating a continuous stream of text. You are writing in short bursts. A CRM note. A follow-up sentence. A property highlight. Push-to-talk handles bursts. Always-on microphones do not.

Use Cases by Task

Property Descriptions

Open your MLS portal or listing software. Click into the description field. Hold the hotkey. Walk through the property highlights from memory or your notes. Release. Edit as needed.

A 300-word property description takes about two minutes to speak. Editing typically takes another three. Total: five minutes. Typing the same description from scratch takes twelve to fifteen minutes.

CRM Notes After Showings

This is where agents lose the most time. You finish a showing, get back to your car, and now you need to capture everything before it fades. Open your laptop or phone (note: dictate.app is Windows-only), dictate your notes while the details are fresh, done.

The push-to-talk design lets you speak a sentence, pause to think, speak another. No need to speak continuously. Think the way you actually think, in fragments.

Client Emails

Most agent emails follow a pattern. Dictating from a mental template is fast. You still personalize each one, but speaking personalization is faster than typing it character by character.

High-volume agents report cutting daily email time from 45-60 minutes to 15-20 minutes after switching to dictation. The math is simple: same words, three times faster.

Offer Letters

Offer letters and cover letters for offers involve significant standard language with personalized details swapped in. Dictating the variable sections while using templates for the fixed sections can cut preparation time substantially.
