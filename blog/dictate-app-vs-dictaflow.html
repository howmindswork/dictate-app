The Word Cap Issue

DictaFlow's 100,000 word/month limit is a hard ceiling. That's roughly 2,000 words/day if you dictate every day. For professionals who dictate email, documentation, Slack, and reports, this constraint hits faster than you'd expect.

At dictate.app, there's no cap. Dictate as much as you want. Your transcription cost is determined by Groq's API pricing (which you control via your own API key), not by our pricing.

Privacy & Architecture

dictate.app uses a BYOK (Bring Your Own Key) model. You provide your Groq API key. Your audio goes directly from your PC to Groq's servers. We never see your audio, never store it, never process it.

DictaFlow routes your audio through their servers. Even if they use Groq on the backend, the intermediate routing adds:

  • Extra latency (audio → DictaFlow → Groq → DictaFlow → you)
  • Privacy exposure (your audio on DictaFlow's infrastructure)
  • Dependency risk (DictaFlow's uptime becomes your uptime)

Speed Matters More Than You Think

~200ms latency (dictate.app) vs ~400ms latency (DictaFlow) doesn't sound like much until you live with it.

At 200ms, dictation feels natural—like speaking into a search engine. You finish your sentence, and the text appears at your cursor almost instantly.

At 400ms, there's a perceptible pause. You're done speaking. You wait. Then the text appears. Over a day of dictating emails, that psychological friction adds up.

Total Cost of Ownership

dictate.app: $8.99/month flat + Groq API costs (typically $0–$5/month for normal use)

DictaFlow: $7/month + hidden cost: when you hit 100k words, you either upgrade, wait, or switch tools.

If you dictate more than ~3,000 words/day on average, DictaFlow forces you to manage a hard limit. dictate.app lets you scale.
